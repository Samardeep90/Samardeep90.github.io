Identity, values, and strengths exploration

Describe your core personal values

Authenticity, compassion, loyalty, harmony, fairness, and equality.

Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made and the consequences of the action you took.

Being power of attorney for my mother and signing a DNR order. She had an incurable, degenerative disease which had already taken years of her life and much of her mobility (throughout which she also battled cancer). She was so frail, her health had come to a stage where her care facility suggested I look at the DNR consent form and talked through whether to sign it or not. In the case that she needed CPR, though it may save her life and keep her going, the compressions required could be painful or traumatic and may leave her in an even worse condition. The chance of her needing the CPR was slim, and I had to think compassionately about what was best for her. I made the decision to sign the DNR. There weren’t consequences as such – no family member disagreed with the decision and when she did pass away it was in her sleep.

In terms of a wider culture I find this quite hard to answer as a Pakeha. Certainly from within family culture I think my values emerged from an environment where they were less than abundant. I think my strengths helped me to survive in that culture, too.

Identify which of your strengths might help you in your learning journey and how they might intersect with learning obstacles.

Attention to detail is great for not just focusing on the big picture of a project but also small facets of it and the minor actions that are required to accomplish the project. However it can clash with time-optimising if I focus on the details for longer than necessary to get the project done. Sometimes just need to make something “good enough” and hit the submit button!
